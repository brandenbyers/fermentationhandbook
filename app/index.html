Praise for The Everyday Fermentation Handbook


  • "Very specific instructions, lovely photos and a fascinating breadth of coverage on ferments of all kinds make The Everyday Fermentation Handbook a book you can actually use every day."
    - Amanda Feifer, founder of Phickle.com

  • "The Everyday Fermentation Handbook is a refreshingly clear guide to the sometimes elusive world of fermentation. From simply elegant (leek rings) to brilliantly bizarre (peanut butter and sauerkraut cream cookies), Branden Byers gives us an honest and creative take on making and using fermented foods at home."
    - Benjamin Wolfe, microbiologist at Harvard University and regular contributor to Lucky Peach

  • "The Everyday Fermentation Handbook is a very accessible introduction to fermentation, with clear, detailed, and straightforward instructions to get you started fermenting all sorts of things at home."
    - Sandor Ellix Katz, Author of The Art of Fermentation and Wild Fermentation

  • "This is a great book for anyone who appreciates this eons-old tradition of food preservation...it provides the reader with the confidence needed to resurrect and make practical (and delicious!) use of these precious cultural traditions."
    - Austin Durant, founder of Fermenters Club

  • "Fermentation is a wonderful and transformative process of food that is easy to embrace for its health-giving properties and exotic taste, but often it can be hard to change our habits to incorporate these in our everyday diet. This is a practical book that gives you inspiration and confidence to do so."
    - Eva Bakkeslett, artist, cultivator and fermenter

  • "Branden's knowledge and excitement about the science and flavors of fermented foods is considerable. I've been lucky to witness this enthusiasm spread to others with his teachings of both beginner and advanced level fermentation topics."
    - Philip Crawford, Slow Food Madison Board Member and Founder of Madison Food Camp

  • "Step by step, the book inspires us with the confidence to experiment and explore new flavors, textures and foods we may not be familiar with. Before you know it, your larder will be filled with delicious, healthy kimchi, heirloom yogurts, sourdough breads, and fermented drinks!
    - Kim Bartko, Program director of Good Food Festival

  • "Newcomers to the world of controlled rot will be inspired into action starting with simple and accessible peaks like sauerkraut and yogurt, but by casting a global net, Byers offers up a world of inspiration for even experienced fermenters."
